Why Vitesse

A new wave of processors — spiking neural networks, neuromorphic chips, analog accelerators — promises orders-of-magnitude efficiency gains for AI workloads. Yet they remain largely inaccessible because standard software stacks were never designed for them.

Vitesse closes that gap. We develop the compiler tooling, model transformation pipelines, and runtime primitives that let AI models exploit the native math of unconventional silicon.

Our long-term vision is to vertically integrate — from open-source software to proprietary chip architectures — and to anchor that effort in Europe. A sovereign, efficient AI compute stack is both a technical and strategic imperative.

We work closely with European research institutions and hardware partners to ensure that leadership in next-generation AI compute is built here.

Approach

Model transformation

Take existing trained models and automatically rewrite them to exploit the primitive operations native to a target processor — stochastic arithmetic, event-driven spikes, in-memory compute.

Compiler & runtime

A hardware-agnostic IR with backend targets for spiking, analog, and custom digital accelerators. Unified toolchain, multiple chips.

Vertical integration

Co-design software and silicon. Insights from the compiler stack inform the architecture of our own future processors, closing the hardware–software loop.

Hardware ecosystem

We develop against a range of novel compute platforms, including neuromorphic and unconventional accelerator architectures from companies such as Etera, Tropic AI, and others emerging from the European deep-tech ecosystem.

Roots

Vitesse is founded by researchers and engineers from EPFL and ETH Zürich, with backgrounds spanning computer architecture, compiler design, and machine learning. We are headquartered in Switzerland and deeply embedded in the European research ecosystem.
